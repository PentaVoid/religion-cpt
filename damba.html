Damba Ayusheev

Who He is

Damba Badmaevich Ayusheev was born September 1, 1962 in Krasnochikoysky District, Chita Oblast, USSR. He serves as the the XXIV Pandito Khambo Lama, the head of the Buddhist Traditional Sangkha of Russia. He was recently re-elected to continue to serve as the head of Russian Buddhists till 2028. Amazingly, he has held this title since 1991. His role is vital to the well being of Buddhism in Russia, which he takes very seriously, to ensure the religion continues to prosper. Similar to other religions, such as christianity, which through the Second Vatican Council modernized the faith, Damba works to ensure Buddhism in Russia is also relevant in this modern world, and not lost in the history books.

Involvement

Sir Ayusheev is also involved in many festivals, one such being the Altargana. This festival is a transborder experience of ethnicity, throughout Mongolia, China, and Russia, although all are welcome. Sir Ayusheev is one of the High patreons of the Altargana, helping organize the many events that occur. The festival attracts thousands of people all around the area, all seeking to enjoy the many events, such as feasts, performances, and games, as well as the sense of community this festival brings. If one is lucky, they may even be able to meet Sir Ayusheev, who also takes the time to enjoy the festival himself.

Importance

Damba Ayusheev does not only play a role in the Buddhist spiritual community, but on a global stage as well. His work as the XXIV Khambo lama has seen him interacting with leaders such as Putin(picture on the right) to put Buddhism in Russia on the map. Thanks to him, Russian Buddhism has not only been recognized, but grown as a result, and his work in the Buddhist Datsans has revolutionized the monasteries teachings, allowing more Buddhists to study and learn in ways alligned with not only the eightfold path, but all Buddhist teachings.
