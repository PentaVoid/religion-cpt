Buddha Worship Ritual - Bayan-Khongor Rock

The Largest Depiction of the Buddha in Russia

Info

The largest image of the Buddha is found in Buryatia. Created in 2006, It is depicted on Bayan-Khongor Rock near the village of Bayan Gol in the Khorinsky District. After the creation of the image in 2006, the area became a popular center for tourists and pilgrims.

Inspiration

The idea for the image of Buddha came from local residents of the area. They asked Tibetan(a.k.a Vajrayana) teacher Venerable Yelo Rinpoche(on the right), the fourth incarnation of Yelo Rinpoche, for help. Earlier, in 1963, The teacher travelled to India where he became a Monk, and to Buryatia in 1993. From there he helped design the image, with minimal impact on the environment, in accordance to the noble eightfold path(Right action).

Ritual

Bayan-Khongor Rock has been considered a sacred place by the local Buddhists for generations.Twice a year, once during the summer, and another during the second day of Saagalan(around February), local Buddhists gather at the Bayan-Khongor Rock. Once there, they enjoy many traditions and prayers at the Rock. All around, there exists small chapels for prayer. There are also sanctuaries containing images of burkhan (Buddhist deities), khurde (prayer drums), lamps, and other items. Although this ritual has many festivities, it is not to be considered a festival, but rather a ritual demonstrating attendance and belief in Buddhist religion.
